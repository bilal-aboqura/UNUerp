# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

UNU ERP primarily serves decision-makers at growing businesses in Saudi Arabia and the wider region: owners, operations leaders, finance leaders, and technology stakeholders evaluating how to replace disconnected business systems. Their job is to find an adaptable platform that can connect customers, sales, accounting, purchasing, inventory, people, projects, and sector-specific workflows without creating another layer of operational complexity.

The website also supports functional leaders researching individual capabilities and organizations in specialized sectors assessing whether UNU can fit their existing workflows.

## Product Purpose

The site explains UNU ERP and its related products, helps prospective customers evaluate fit across capabilities and industries, and converts qualified interest into a personalized demo or consultation. Success means a visitor understands the value of one connected operational platform, recognizes that it can adapt to their organization, and starts a conversation with an ERP expert.

## Positioning

UNU gives growing regional businesses one open, adaptable source of operational truth, combining ERP breadth with specialized products and implementation shaped around real workflows rather than rigid software constraints.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Request a personalized demo or talk to an ERP expert.
- Secondary CTA: Explore features, products, industries, and custom pricing before contacting the team.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: One connected, open platform for the work that runs your business.
- Belief ladder: disconnected tools create friction; shared real-time data improves decisions; UNU connects core functions in one system; open-source control and configurable workflows prevent lock-in; a personalized implementation can fit the visitor's operation.
- Proof on hand: six client-logo image assets in `public/assets/`, a Riyadh presence, broad module coverage, 21 listed industries, and five specialized UNU products. The repository does not identify the logo owners or contain testimonials, case studies, quantified outcomes, or third-party validation, so the site should not imply more proof than is available.

## Brand Personality

Confident, pragmatic, and empowering. The voice should make enterprise software feel understandable and controllable: direct about operational problems, concrete about connected workflows, and optimistic about growth without sounding inflated or overly technical. The experience should communicate regional relevance and human support while remaining credible to business and technology stakeholders. English and Arabic are both present; the current repository establishes English as the complete primary experience and Arabic as an emerging localized experience.

## Anti-references

Avoid the visual and verbal language of rigid legacy ERP: dense feature dumps, intimidating technical jargon, bureaucratic navigation, and interfaces that suggest long training cycles. Avoid generic SaaS promises, unsupported superlatives, anonymous social proof presented as authority, and messaging centered on seat counts or closed-platform dependency. Do not make the brand feel like a thin reskin of a global ERP vendor or reduce regional relevance to decorative cultural motifs.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with operational outcomes, then substantiate them with connected capabilities.
2. Make breadth feel coherent rather than overwhelming by showing how teams share one source of truth.
3. Pair enterprise credibility with plain language and a clear path to a human consultation.
4. Demonstrate adaptability through real workflows, industries, and deployment choices instead of vague customization claims.
5. Earn trust through specific, verifiable proof and treat Arabic localization as a first-class experience as it expands.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support responsive keyboard-friendly web use, visible focus states, semantic structure, readable contrast, reduced-motion preferences, and bidirectional English/Arabic content. No formal WCAG conformance target or documented user-specific accessibility requirement exists in the repository; do not claim certification until the site has been audited and remediated against an agreed standard.
